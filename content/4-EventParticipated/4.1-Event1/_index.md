### Event Objectives

- Update participants on the latest trends in Artificial Intelligence (AI), Cloud Computing, and career development in the technology industry.
- Share effective learning strategies, self-discipline, and personal development methods in a technical environment.
- Introduce practical approaches to applying AI in software development and project management.
- Create networking opportunities among the FCAJ community, AWS Study Group, and industry partners.

### Key Highlights

#### Motivation and Learning Habits

- The speakers explained why many learners lose focus due to social media and entertainment platforms.
- Several practical techniques were introduced to improve concentration, including breaking study sessions into smaller blocks, building daily habits, and rewarding yourself after completing learning goals.

#### Overcoming the Amygdala with Chunking

- Learners should understand the fundamentals before relying on AI tools to avoid dependency and inaccurate results.
- Employers continue to value problem-solving skills more than simply knowing how to use AI tools.

#### Hiring Mindset and Strong Foundations

- Employers highly value analytical thinking and a solid technical foundation.
- AI should be considered an amplifier that improves productivity for skilled people rather than a replacement for knowledge.
- Integrity and personal responsibility remain essential when working with AI-assisted development.

#### BMX Development Method

- The speakers introduced the BMX methodology, which divides large software projects into manageable tasks such as Epics and User Stories.
- Different AI Agent roles (Project Manager, Architect, Developer, Reviewer) were presented together with the importance of well-prepared documentation to reduce AI hallucination and improve collaboration.

#### Rank System and Habit Building

- The community encourages continuous learning through a ranking system based on technical skills and practical achievements.
- Participants were introduced to the concept of maintaining daily learning streaks, similar to language-learning applications, to build long-term discipline.
